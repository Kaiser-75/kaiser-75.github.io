Kaiser Hamid

I am a researcher working at the intersection of Human–Computer Interaction (HCI) and autonomous driving. My work focuses on understanding and predicting driver behavior (e.g., attention, intent, and future actions) using multimodal perception and learning-based methods.

Broadly, I am interested in robust, interpretable, and scalable approaches for end-to-end driving and driver-intent prediction, including vision-language models, temporal reasoning, and evaluation protocols that generalize across datasets and scenarios.

I am open to research collaborations and internship/visiting opportunities. If you think there is a fit, please reach out.

Research

My research spans driver intent prediction, human-centered autonomy, and multimodal learning for autonomous driving. I study how to leverage vision, temporal context, and (when available) language- or gaze-based supervision to build models that are accurate, generalizable, and explainable in safety-critical settings.
